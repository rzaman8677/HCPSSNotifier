American Underdog Movie Review

Last updated: 3/7/2023 by Raiyan Zaman

American Underdog is a biographical sports drama film directed by the Erwin Brothers and starring Zachary Levi as NFL quarterback Kurt Warner. The film chronicles Warner's rise from a grocery store stockboy to a Super Bowl MVP, focusing on his faith, perseverance, and determination to overcome the many obstacles he faced on his journey to success.

The film opens with Warner (Levi) as a young man working in a grocery store in Iowa, dreaming of becoming a professional football player. Warner's dreams are dashed when he fails to make it onto his college football team and is forced to give up on his dreams. Despite this setback, Warner remains determined to pursue his passion and eventually lands a spot on the Arena Football League's Iowa Barnstormers.

As Warner begins to make a name for himself in the AFL, he meets and falls in love with Brenda (Anna Paquin), a single mother with two children. Together, they face many challenges, including financial difficulties and opposition from Brenda's father, who disapproves of Kurt's lack of financial stability.

Despite the many obstacles in his way, Warner refuses to give up on his dream and continues to work tirelessly to improve his game. His perseverance pays off when he is signed to play for the NFL's St. Louis Rams, where he becomes the starting quarterback and leads his team to a Super Bowl victory.

American Underdog is a moving and inspiring film that tells a powerful story of faith, perseverance, and overcoming adversity. The film's themes of determination, hard work, and faith will resonate with audiences of all ages and backgrounds.

One of the strengths of the film is its portrayal of Kurt Warner as a relatable and likable character. Zachary Levi delivers a strong performance as Warner, capturing the quarterback's humility, determination, and faith. Anna Paquin also delivers a solid performance as Brenda, portraying her character's strength and resilience in the face of adversity.

The film's pacing is strong, with the story unfolding at a steady and engaging pace. The film does an excellent job of balancing the personal and professional aspects of Warner's story, showing how his faith and family life influenced his career and helped him overcome the many obstacles he faced.

The film's production values are also impressive. The football scenes are well-shot and visually stunning, capturing the intensity and excitement of the game. The film's use of music is also effective, with a strong score and well-chosen soundtrack that enhances the emotional impact of the story.

One of the film's main themes is the power of faith in overcoming adversity. The film portrays Kurt Warner's faith as an essential part of his success, showing how it helped him overcome the many obstacles he faced on his journey to the NFL. While some viewers may find the film's emphasis on faith heavy-handed, the portrayal of faith in the film is never preachy or overly sentimental.

In terms of criticism, some viewers may find the film's portrayal of Warner's career overly simplistic. While the film does touch on the many obstacles and setbacks Warner faced on his journey, it does not delve into the complexities of the NFL or the challenges faced by other players. Additionally, some viewers may find the film's depiction of Warner's relationship with Brenda somewhat idealized, with little exploration of the difficulties they faced as a couple.

Overall, American Underdog is a touching and inspiring film that tells the incredible true story of one of the greatest football players of all time. The film's strong performances, compelling storytelling, and well-executed football scenes make it a must-watch for fans of sports dramas and anyone who loves a good underdog story.

The film's message about the power of perseverance and the importance of having a strong support system is a timeless one that is sure to resonate with audiences of all ages. It is a film that celebrates the human spirit and the triumph of the human will over adversity.

While the film does have its flaws, particularly in its pacing and occasional moments of clichéd dialogue, it is ultimately a strong and inspiring film that is well worth watching. If you're a fan of football or inspiring true stories, you won't want to miss American Underdog.
