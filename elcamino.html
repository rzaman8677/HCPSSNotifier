El Camino Movie Review

Last updated: 3/12/2023 by Andrew Song

El Camino is a 2019 film directed by Vince Gilligan and serves as a continuation of the hit television series Breaking Bad. The film follows the character of Jesse Pinkman, played by Aaron Paul, in the aftermath of the series finale, as he attempts to start a new life while being pursued by the law and his own traumatic past. El Camino is a gritty and emotional film that offers a satisfying conclusion to one of the most beloved characters in television history.

The film's cinematography is one of its standout elements, with the same high-quality visual style as the television series. The film's use of natural lighting and gritty camera work adds to the tension and drama of the story, and the desert landscape of New Mexico serves as a striking backdrop for the film's action. The film's attention to detail and continuity with the television series is also impressive, with the same iconic locations and character appearances that fans of the show will recognize and appreciate.

Aaron Paul's performance as Jesse Pinkman is another highlight of the film. Paul's ability to convey emotion and vulnerability while still portraying the toughness and grit of his character is a testament to his skill as an actor. Paul's performance captures the pain and trauma that Jesse has experienced throughout the series and makes the character's journey in the film feel all the more compelling.

The supporting cast of the film is also impressive, with several returning actors from the series reprising their roles. Characters like Skinny Pete and Badger provide much-needed moments of levity in an otherwise tense and emotional film, while characters like Todd and Mike add to the overall complexity of the story. The new characters introduced in the film are also well-acted, with characters like Ed the Disappearer and Neil adding to the film's overall tension and drama.

The film's script is well-written, with a story that manages to be both a satisfying conclusion to Jesse's story and a standalone film in its own right. The film's exploration of themes like redemption and trauma is executed in a way that feels organic and true to the character of Jesse, and the film's pacing and structure allow for moments of both tension and reflection. The film's climax, in particular, is a standout moment that provides closure to both Jesse's story and the overall Breaking Bad universe.

One of the few flaws of the film is that it may not be as accessible to viewers who have not seen the original series. While the film does offer some context and backstory for those who may be unfamiliar with Breaking Bad, much of the emotional weight and significance of the film's events may be lost on those who have not followed the series. Additionally, some fans of the series may find the film's pacing to be slow at times, as the film takes its time in exploring Jesse's emotional journey.

Another issue with the film is that it may not provide closure for all fans of the series. While the film does offer a satisfying conclusion to Jesse's story, it may not tie up all of the loose ends or provide answers to all of the questions that fans may have had. However, this is a minor complaint, as the film's primary focus is on Jesse's emotional journey rather than providing a comprehensive explanation of the series' events.

Overall, El Camino is a well-crafted and emotional film that offers a satisfying conclusion to the character of Jesse Pinkman. The film's visual style, performances, and script all come together to create a tense and compelling story that will satisfy fans of the series and newcomers alike. El Camino is a worthy addition to the Breaking Bad universe and a testament to the talent of Vince Gilligan and the cast and crew involved in its production.
