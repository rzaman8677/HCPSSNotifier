Jurassic World Movie Review

Last updated: 3/11/2023 by Raiyan Zaman

Jurassic World is a 2015 science-fiction adventure film directed by Colin Trevorrow and produced by Steven Spielberg. It is the fourth installment in the Jurassic Park franchise and a direct sequel to the original film. Set 22 years after the events of the first film, the movie takes place on Isla Nublar, where a new theme park called Jurassic World has been built.

The film stars Chris Pratt as Owen Grady, a dinosaur trainer, and Bryce Dallas Howard as Claire Dearing, the park's operations manager. The two lead characters have a complicated history, which is revealed throughout the film. The plot revolves around the creation of a genetically modified dinosaur called Indominus Rex, which was designed to be bigger, stronger, and scarier than any dinosaur before it. When the Indominus Rex escapes from its enclosure, Owen and Claire must work together to stop the creature before it causes chaos and destruction on the island.

Jurassic World is a visually stunning film that features breathtaking scenery and realistic special effects. The CGI is expertly done, and the dinosaurs look incredibly lifelike. The film's action sequences are intense and thrilling, and the tension builds throughout the film as the Indominus Rex wreaks havoc on the island.

Chris Pratt delivers a standout performance as Owen Grady. He's a charming and charismatic leading man who brings humor and heart to the film. Bryce Dallas Howard is also excellent as Claire Dearing. While her character starts off as a bit of a stereotype, she becomes more nuanced as the film progresses. The supporting cast is also strong, with Vincent D'Onofrio as the head of security and Irrfan Khan as the park's owner.

One of the film's strengths is its exploration of the theme park concept. The movie asks questions about the ethics of creating genetically modified creatures for entertainment purposes, as well as the potential dangers of playing God. It also comments on the modern obsession with consumerism and the need for ever-bigger and more spectacular attractions. The film is a commentary on the spectacle-driven nature of modern society and the consequences that come with trying to create bigger and better things.

The film's pacing is generally strong, with a good balance of action and character development. However, the film does have some pacing issues in the middle act, where the focus on character development and exposition slows down the action. The film also suffers from some clunky dialogue, particularly in the early scenes.

The film's main villain, the Indominus Rex, is an impressive creation. It's a frightening and imposing creature that dominates the screen whenever it's on screen. However, the film also suffers from a lack of variety in its dinosaur cast. While the Indominus Rex is an impressive new creation, most of the other dinosaurs in the film are recycled from previous Jurassic Park movies.

Overall, Jurassic World is an entertaining and visually stunning film that successfully updates the Jurassic Park franchise for a new generation. The film is at its best when it's focused on the action and the dinosaurs, which are the real stars of the show. The film's exploration of the theme park concept is interesting, but the film is at its weakest when it tries to delve too deeply into character development and exposition. Nonetheless, the film is a must-see for fans of the franchise and anyone who loves dinosaurs and action movies.
