Lightyear Movie Review

Last updated: 3/6/2023 by Raiyan Zaman

Lightyear is a 2022 science-fiction animated film produced by Pixar Animation Studios and directed by Angus MacLane. It is a prequel to the Toy Story franchise and follows the origin story of the character Buzz Lightyear, who is voiced by Chris Evans. The film takes place in a different universe than the Toy Story films and presents a more mature and action-packed version of the character.

The plot of the film centers around the young test pilot Buzz Lightyear, who is recruited by a space agency to join a team of elite astronauts on a mission to stop an evil dictator from taking over the galaxy. Along the way, Buzz encounters a cast of unique characters, including a mysterious warrior named Typhy and a rogue smuggler named Wildcard, who becomes his unlikely alliy.

The animation in Lightyear is stunning, with beautifully rendered environments and character designs. The film's space sequences are particularly impressive, with thrilling dogfights and breathtaking visuals. The action sequences are intense and exciting, with a level of adrenaline-pumping energy that is rare in animated films. The film also features a fantastic soundtrack, which adds to the overall cinematic experience.

Chris Evans delivers an excellent performance as Buzz Lightyear. He brings a level of gravitas to the character, which is different from the comedic tone of the Toy Story films. The rest of the voice cast is also impressive, with Zoe Saldana as Typhy, Mark Hamill as the evil dictator, and Olivia Munn as Wildcard.

One of the strengths of the film is its exploration of the science-fiction genre. The film takes inspiration from classic sci-fi films and TV shows, including Star Wars, Star Trek, and Battlestar Galactica, and combines them with the Toy Story universe to create a unique and compelling story. The film's world-building is excellent, with a rich history and mythology that expands on the Toy Story universe.

The film's pacing is generally strong, with a good balance of action and character development. The film's runtime of 107 minutes is just the right length to tell the story without dragging or feeling rushed. The film's writing is also excellent, with clever dialogue and well-crafted set pieces.

One of the few drawbacks of the film is its lack of emotional resonance. While the film has a few moments of emotional weight, it never quite reaches the emotional depth of the Toy Story films. This is partly due to the fact that the film is a prequel, and audiences already know the fate of the character, but it also feels like the film prioritizes action and spectacle over character development.

Another issue with the film is that it feels somewhat disconnected from the Toy Story franchise. While the film is a prequel to the Toy Story films, it doesn't really feel like it belongs in the same universe. This is partly due to the fact that the film is more mature and action-packed than the Toy Story films, but it also feels like the film is trying to distance itself from the franchise to establish its own identity.

Despite these minor flaws, Lightyear is a fantastic film that offers a fresh and exciting take on the Buzz Lightyear character. The film's action sequences are some of the best in any Pixar film, and the animation is breathtaking. The film's exploration of the science-fiction genre is excellent, and the voice cast is superb. While the film may not have the emotional depth of the Toy Story films, it more than makes up for it with its thrilling action and expert storytelling. Overall, Lightyear is a must-see for Pixar fans and anyone who loves science-fiction and action movies.
