Rocky Movie Review

Last updated: 2/25/2023 by Raiyan Zaman

Rocky is a 1976 American sports drama film directed by John G. Avildsen and written by and starring Sylvester Stallone. The film tells the story of Rocky Balboa, a small-time boxer from Philadelphia who gets a shot at the heavyweight championship of the world. The film is a classic underdog story that has become a cultural icon, with iconic moments, memorable characters, and a powerful message about determination and hard work.

One of the standout elements of Rocky is Stallone's performance as the titular character. Stallone's portrayal of Rocky is both nuanced and powerful, capturing the character's mix of toughness and vulnerability. Rocky is a character who is easy to root for, and Stallone's performance makes the audience invested in his journey. The chemistry between Stallone and Talia Shire, who plays Rocky's love interest Adrian, is also a highlight of the film. Their relationship is sweet and sincere, adding an emotional depth to the film.

The film's soundtrack is another standout element, with Bill Conti's iconic score becoming synonymous with the film itself. The use of music throughout the film is powerful, with the score underscoring the emotional moments and elevating the action sequences. The film's use of popular songs, including Survivor's "Eye of the Tiger" and Bill Withers' "Ain't No Sunshine," is also excellent, adding to the film's overall impact.

The film's cinematography is also noteworthy, with the use of long tracking shots and handheld cameras giving the film a sense of realism and urgency. The fight scenes are particularly impressive, with Avildsen's direction making the audience feel like they are in the ring with Rocky. The use of close-ups and slow-motion shots adds to the intensity of the fights, making them some of the most memorable moments of the film.

One of the strengths of Rocky is its message about determination and hard work. The film is a testament to the idea that anyone can achieve their dreams if they are willing to put in the effort. Rocky is not a natural talent, but his hard work and dedication pay off in the end. The film's message is inspiring and has resonated with audiences for over four decades.

The film's supporting characters are also well-developed, with Burgess Meredith's performance as Rocky's trainer Mickey being a standout. Meredith's performance is both tough and tender, with his scenes with Stallone providing some of the film's most emotional moments. Carl Weathers' performance as Apollo Creed, Rocky's opponent in the climactic fight, is also excellent. Weathers brings charisma and energy to the role that makes Creed a worthy adversary for Rocky.

One of the few flaws of the film is that it can feel a bit slow at times. The film takes its time to develop the characters and their relationships, which can make the pacing drag in certain scenes. This is a minor complaint, however, and doesn't detract from the overall impact of the film.

Another issue with the film is that it may not be as appealing to younger audiences as it is to older ones. The film's pacing and overall tone may not be as exciting or engaging for viewers who are used to more fast-paced and action-packed films. That being said, the film's message and themes are universal and can resonate with viewers of any age.

Overall, Rocky is a classic film that has stood the test of time. The film's performances, soundtrack, cinematography, and message are all excellent, with a level of heart and emotion that is rare in sports films. The film's underdog story has become a cultural touchstone, inspiring countless filmmakers and athletes. Rocky is a film that everyone should see at least once, and it is a testament to the power of determination and hard work.
