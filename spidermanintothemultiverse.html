Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse Movie Review

Last updated: 2/27/2023 by Raiyan Zaman

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is a groundbreaking animated movie that tells the story of Miles Morales (Shameik Moore), a teenager who gains spider-like powers and becomes the newest Spider-Man in a multiverse of alternate dimensions. Directed by Bob Persichetti, Peter Ramsey, and Rodney Rothman, the movie is a visually stunning and emotionally resonant superhero adventure that will thrill audiences of all ages.

One of the most striking things about the movie is its animation style. The movie combines traditional animation techniques with modern computer-generated effects, resulting in a visually stunning and unique look that feels like a comic book coming to life. The animation is full of vibrant colors, bold lines, and dynamic camera angles, making every frame feel like a work of art.

The movie's story is equally impressive, as it weaves together multiple threads and characters from the Spider-Man comics. The movie introduces us to a host of alternate versions of Spider-Man, including an older Peter Parker (Jake Johnson), a Spider-Woman from another dimension (Hailee Steinfeld), and even an animated pig named Spider-Ham (John Mulaney). Each of these characters brings a unique personality and backstory to the movie, and they all play an important role in the story.

At its core, the movie is about Miles Morales and his journey to becoming a hero. Miles is a relatable and likable protagonist, struggling with the pressures of high school, his relationships with his family and friends, and the burden of being Spider-Man. The movie does a great job of exploring Miles' character, and his journey is both emotional and inspiring.

The movie's action scenes are some of the most impressive ever seen in a superhero movie. The characters move with incredible speed and agility, and the animation is so fluid and dynamic that it's easy to get caught up in the excitement of the moment. Each character has a unique fighting style, and the battles are choreographed in a way that highlights their strengths and weaknesses.

Another standout aspect of the movie is its music. The movie's soundtrack is a mix of hip-hop, R&B, and pop, with artists like Post Malone, Swae Lee, and Jaden Smith contributing original songs. The music adds another layer of energy and emotion to the movie, and it's a perfect fit for the movie's urban setting and diverse cast of characters.

The voice acting in the movie is also excellent, with each actor bringing a unique spin to their character. Shameik Moore is great as Miles Morales, bringing a lot of heart and soul to the character. Jake Johnson is also fantastic as Peter Parker, playing the character of a jaded and slightly broken hero who's seen better days. The supporting cast is equally strong, with Hailee Steinfeld, Mahershala Ali, and Brian Tyree Henry all turning in memorable performances.

If there's one area where the movie falls short, it's in its villain. The movie's main antagonist, Kingpin (Liev Schreiber), feels a bit underdeveloped compared to the richly drawn heroes. However, this is a minor quibble, as the movie's focus is on its heroes and their journey, rather than on the villain.

Overall, Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is a remarkable achievement in animation and superhero storytelling. It's a movie that breaks new ground in terms of its visual style and narrative scope while also delivering a heartfelt and inspiring story about what it means to be a hero. The movie is a must-see for fans of Spider-Man, animation, or superhero movies in general, and it's a movie that will be remembered for years to come.
