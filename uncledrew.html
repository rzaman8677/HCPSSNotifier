Uncle Drew Movie Review

Last updated: 3/9/2023 by Raiyan Zaman

Uncle Drew is a 2018 sports comedy film that was directed by Charles Stone III and written by Jay Longino. The movie starred NBA player Kyrie Irving as Uncle Drew, an elderly basketball player who reunites with his old teammates to compete in a streetball tournament.

The film begins with a brief introduction to Uncle Drew, an aging basketball player who has become a legend in the streetball community. Uncle Drew is renowned for his skills on the court, as well as his quirky personality and old-school approach to the game. When his old rival Mookie (Nick Kroll) challenges him to a streetball tournament in Harlem, Drew decides to reunite with his former teammates and compete in the tournament.

The first half of the film is dedicated to introducing the various characters that make up Drew's team. Each player has a unique personality and backstory that is explored throughout the film. For example, Preacher (Chris Webber) is a former basketball star who has turned to preaching, while Boots (Nate Robinson) is a player with a prosthetic leg. The film does an excellent job of balancing comedy and drama, as each character struggles with their own personal demons and insecurities.

One of the standout performances in the film comes from Lil Rel Howery, who plays Dax, the coach of Drew's team. Dax is a young, ambitious basketball coach who dreams of winning the tournament and proving himself to his ex-girlfriend. Howery's comedic timing and delivery are spot-on, and he manages to bring a lot of heart to the character as well.

The film really picks up in the second half, as the tournament gets underway, and the team faces off against a variety of opponents. The basketball scenes are well choreographed and exciting, with some impressive displays of athleticism from the actors. The film also does an excellent job of incorporating some of the classic moves and styles from old-school basketball into the gameplay.

As the tournament progresses, the team faces a number of setbacks, both on and off the court. These moments of adversity help to bring the characters closer together, and there are some genuinely touching moments as they learn to work as a team and support each other. The film also touches on some deeper themes, such as the importance of friendship, forgiveness, and perseverance.

One of the weaker aspects of the film is the antagonist, played by Nick Kroll. Mookie is a one-dimensional character who serves mainly as a foil for Drew and his team. While Kroll does a good job with the material he's given, the character ultimately feels underdeveloped and lacks the depth of the other characters.

Overall, Uncle Drew is a fun and entertaining film that manages to balance comedy, drama, and sports action. While it may not be the most original or groundbreaking film, it succeeds in its goal of delivering a crowd-pleasing experience. The film's talented cast, engaging story, and impressive basketball scenes make it a must-see for fans of the genre.

The film's central theme is the power of friendship and teamwork. Drew's team is made up of a diverse group of characters who each bring their own unique skills and personalities to the game. The team members are initially reluctant to play together and have difficulty working as a cohesive unit. However, as the tournament progresses, they learn to put their differences aside and work together towards a common goal.

The film also touches on the importance of forgiveness and redemption. Many of the characters have made mistakes in their past and are seeking a chance to make things right. Drew, in particular, is haunted by a mistake he made years ago that led to the dissolution of his team. The film shows how the characters are able to overcome their past mistakes and move forward by working together and supporting each other.

Another theme that the film explores is the tension between old-school and new-school basketball. Drew and his team represent the old-school style of basketball, characterized by fundamentals, teamwork, and a slower pace. In contrast, their opponents in the tournament are younger players who rely more on flash and individual skill. The film uses this conflict to highlight the strengths and weaknesses of both styles of play, and ultimately shows that there is room for both in the world of basketball.

One of the strengths of the film is its cast of talented actors and basketball players. Kyrie Irving is impressive in his portrayal of Uncle Drew, bringing a lot of personality and charm to the character. He also showcases his skills on the court, performing some impressive moves and demonstrating his athleticism. The rest of the team is also well-cast, with each actor bringing their own unique energy and comedic timing to their role. Chris Webber is a standout as Preacher, delivering some of the film's funniest moments with his over-the-top preaching.

The basketball scenes in the film are a highlight, with some impressive choreography and editing. The filmmakers do an excellent job of capturing the excitement and energy of streetball, while also incorporating some of the classic moves and styles of old-school basketball. The film also features a number of celebrity cameos, including Shaquille O'Neal, Reggie Miller, and Lisa Leslie, who all bring their own basketball expertise to the film.

While Uncle Drew is primarily a comedy and sports film, it also touches on some deeper themes and emotions. The film explores the concept of legacy and what it means to leave a lasting impact on the world. Drew is a character who is grappling with his own legacy, and the film shows how he is able to reconcile his past mistakes and make a positive impact on the lives of others.

Another theme that the film explores is the power of community. The streetball community in Harlem is shown as a tight-knit group that values loyalty and respect. The film depicts how this community comes together to support each other and celebrate the game of basketball. This theme is particularly resonant in today's society, where communities are often fractured and divided.

Overall, Uncle Drew is a fun and engaging film that succeeds in its goal of delivering an entertaining sports comedy. While it may not be the most groundbreaking or innovative film, it manages to balance comedy, drama, and sports action in a way that is sure to please audiences. The film's talented cast, engaging story, and impressive basketball scenes make it a must-see for fans of the genre.
